**Cluster 6 Synthesis for Policy Copilot:** The trust literature reveals a 30-year arc from Lee & Moray's trust–control model to modern trust-adaptive interventions. The critical conflict is between "XAI helps" and "XAI doesn't help" camps. Vasconcelos (2023) resolves this: explanations help *only when they reduce verification cost sufficiently*. Policy Copilot's citation-with-highlight design is defensible precisely because it lowers verification cost (users click to see the exact policy passage), but the verifiability gap critique (Schemmer 2023) warns against overclaiming. The forced uncertainty disclosure aligns with Buçinca's cognitive forcing evidence.

**Cluster 10 Synthesis for Policy Copilot:** The LSEP landscape has shifted dramatically since Strubell (2019). Three developments matter: (1) inference now dominates lifecycle emissions (60–90%), reframing the environmental conversation from training to deployment; (2) water consumption has emerged as a parallel concern alongside carbon; (3) the transparency–automation incompatibility (Zarsky 2016) is a structural constraint that Policy Copilot's decomposable retrieval pipeline partially resolves. The Green AI vs. performance debate is largely resolved at the architectural level—RAG inherently aligns with Green AI by externalising knowledge to retrieval rather than storing it in parameters—but per-query inference costs remain a legitimate LSEP concern.
